Modeling Commodity Futures with Realized Volatility

In light of the recent crisis in the European energy system, we propose ananalysis of futures contracts written on commodities representative of thekey factors of production in the EU economy: Dutch TTF, Brent crude oil- for its ties to inflation and economic activity - and Carbon Allowancesof the European Trading Scheme - for their role in the sustainable transition,as production costs for polluting firms. We take innovative methods,recently introduced for equity indexes and successful in capturing theirvolatility, and apply them to commodity futures. They take as input highfrequencytrade data, which is used to compute a measure of daily realizedvolatility that incorporates, by construction, information about intra-daytransactions. We test the realized volatility for its power in explaining dailyclose-to-close returns volatility, to understand if the data-rich model, as happensin the equity market, brings enough benefits to justify its use. We findthat, for the commodity futures under study, the answer is negative, basedon a Value-at-Risk validation test. We also blend this frontier method withclassical univariate and multivariate financial time-series modeling. The realizedvolatility is included as an exogenous regressor inside multiple differentunivariate GARCH(p,q) model specifications. We find that the realizedvolatility consistently improves the quality of the fit and is a significant explanatoryfactor of the close-to-close volatility, but its predictive power isunsatisfactory. In the multivariate setting the interaction terms between thevariances of all commodities are included and we are able to highlight thesimultaneous transmission channels between them..
